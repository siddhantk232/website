General Tips

In a way these two ideas relate to Goodhart's law. How many problems you have solved is an ok measure for how much effort you have put in, but if it becomes your goal, then you are by human nature likely to solve a lot of problems in the easiest way possible which of course defeats the purpose of practicing.

  • PRACTICE TO LEARN! not mark someting off the todo list or check off daily routine/target. Learn from every problem you solve.
  • Understand and improve thought process (try to come up with alternate solutions).
  • DO NOT MEMORISE SOLUTIONS!
  • Challenge your mind and improve. DON'T just solve problems daily for the sake of solving them.
  • Try to solve topic based questions on the topic you just learnt (from hackerrank?).
  • BYOT: Build Your Own Template (utilities that work in all questions).
  • Sit with a pen and paper!
  • Don't wast time on useless stuff (like this video I'm watching).

Focus!

This is the most important thing that I want to get out of doing CP. Having a clear mind is gonna help me for sure in future in deciding side projects and keep focus until I finish them. Ask questions like:

  • why am I doing this?
  • why is this important?
  • why am I wathcing this video or reading this shit?
  • what do I need this information for?

Mistakes

  • When there's no solution hiding anywhere. Don't be super stuck.
  • Not fixing your weaknesses.
  • Don't be afraid of being inconsistent.
  • Don't jump immeidately into writing code.
  • Not looking for edge cases.
  • Misreading/reading too fast.
  • Reading theory/code more than practicing.
